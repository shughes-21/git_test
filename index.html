What is MSUD

MSUD is a rare life threatening autosomal recessive disorder caused by the body's inability to efficiently process branched chain amino acids (BCAA'S).

How common is MSUD

MSUD affects 1 in 185,000 babies born world wide.

What is the Cause of MSUD?

MSUD is caused by a genetic variants in the BCKDHA, BCKDHB, or DBT genes which affects the subunit of an enzyme required to process the branched chain amino acids (BCAA's)

What are the BCAA's

  • Leucine
  • Isoleucine
  • Valine
